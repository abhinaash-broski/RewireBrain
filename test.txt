Thank you for your inquiry. 

This information applies only if you:

reside within the jurisdiction of the German Embassy in Kathmandu (Gyaneshwor Marg 690), responsible for all of Nepal. (If you reside somewhere else, please find the competent mission here),
want to study in Germany at a university (or equivalent institution) and intend to stay in Germany for more than 90 days (otherwise, please find the correct visa category in our Visa Navigator).
 

In the following, we provide general information about obtaining a student visa for Germany. If your question is not fully addressed by the information below, please reply to this message, and we will get back to you as soon as possible.

 

 

IF YOU WANT TO APPLY FOR A VISA:

Please refer to the following websites for detailed information on the application process and appointments:

Online application national visa
Study visa 
Appointment portal
Regarding visa fees and the submission of necessary documents, please refer to the checklist given on our website. 

All applicants have to register and upload all necessary documents in the appointment portal (Auslandsportal).

IF YOU HAVE ALREADY APPLIED FOR A VISA:

The standard processing time for student visa applications (including preparatory language courses) is 12 weeks. Kindly understand that we cannot provide individual status updates within this time frame. If the Embassy has requested you to provide additional document(s), your visa application remains on hold until you submit the document(s). This time does not count towards the standard processing time.

If the standard processing time has already passed and you would like to enquire about the status of your application, please reply to this e-mail with, at least, the following information:

Last/family name, first/given name, written as in passport
passport number
citizenship of applicant
birthdate of applicant
date when application was made
if known: application no. („Barcode“ or „BC number“)
If your joining date at the university (deadline for enrollment) has passed or is about to pass, please provide a confirmation letter of your university stating that you will be able to join the class later.

 

IF YOUR VISA APPLICATION WAS DECLINED:

You have two options: 1. file a lawsuit in the administrative court in Berlin (please check your refusal notice for details), or 2. file a new visa application. Since 1st January 2024, the legal remedy of „remonstration“ is no longer available.

 

DE-BLOCKING OF BLOCKED ACCOUNT

If you no longer want to or are able to travel to Germany with your student visa, you can request the Embassy to cancel your visa. After cancellation, we can issue you a consular certificate confirming your visa has been cancelled that you can show to your blocked account provider to close your blocked account and receive your blocked money back. To obtain such a certificate, please visit the Embassy on Tuesdays or Thursdays between 8am and 10am.
